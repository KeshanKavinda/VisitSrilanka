Srilankan Foods

Sri Lanka, a beautiful island nation in South Asia, boasts a rich culinary heritage with a diverse range of street foods, traditional dishes, and cultural foods. Sri Lankan cuisine is influenced by various cultures, including Sinhalese, Tamil, Muslim, and Malay, creating a unique and flavorful gastronomic experience. Here are some popular street foods, traditional foods, and cultural foods of Sri Lanka

Foods of Srilanka

Traditional Foods

  • Kottu Roti

Perhaps the most famous Sri Lankan street food, Kottu Roti is a dish made from shredded flatbread (roti) stir-fried with vegetables, eggs, and a choice of meat or seafood. The sounds of the rhythmic chopping of the roti by the street vendors are characteristic of this dish.

  • Hoppers (Appa)

Hoppers are bowl-shaped, crispy-edged pancakes made from fermented rice flour and coconut milk. They can be served as plain hoppers or with eggs (egg hoppers) or even sweet jaggery (pittu).

  • Isso Wade (Prawn Fritters)

Isso wade are deep-fried spicy lentil patties with whole prawns embedded in them. They are a popular snack, especially in coastal regions.

Cultural Foods

  • Watalappam

A delightful dessert of Malay origin, wattalappam is a rich, creamy custard made from jaggery, coconut milk, eggs, and various spices.

  • Achcharu

A type of pickle or relish made from a mix of fruits and vegetables, achcharu is a popular accompaniment to meals in Sri Lanka..
